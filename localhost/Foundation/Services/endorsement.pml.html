Process and guidelines to get an interim endorsement from the Foundation

As explained here, the Foundation is working with members on developing certification programs for services, training and products. Until these are in place, we will provide provisional endorsements, which will lapse once the certification schemes are in place. We will support members with provisional endorsements in achieving full certification.

Services

  • The certification (or endorsement for now) will indicate that a particular provider has the skills and experience to provide high-quality services to adopters of seL4.
  • Guidelines:
    • The seL4 Compliance Committee (CC) will award Interim Endorsement of an seL4 Service Provider based on an assessment that the provider is:
      • known and respected in the seL4 Community;
      • has demonstrated technical competence in the development of code or proofs of the seL4 kernel or software components running directly the seL4 kernel or tools supporting the development of seL4-based systems;
      • has a track record of contributions to the seL4 ecosystem.
      The CC may seek opinions from representatives of the Foundation’s Technical Steering Committee (TSC).
    • A developer/provider of a Training Module may request Interim Endorsement by contacting the CC via email, giving at least three weeks warning prior to providing the actual material.
    • The CC will provides its assessment within two weeks of receiving the material. The assessment may either be an unconditional endorsement, or an conditional endorsement pending some required changes, or a list of recommended changes for a new revised submission. The assessment may be based on presentation 1 material alone but may also include an examination of any code provided for hands-on training, at the discretion of the CC.
    • All endorsed training modules will be advertised here.
    • Interim Endorsement will be provided for free and to members only.

Training

  • The certification (or endorsement for now) will indicate that a particular training is of high quality and effective in providing useful skills for deploying the seL4 microkernel.
  • Guidelines:
    • The seL4 Compliance Committee (CC) will award Interim Endorsement of an seL4 Training Module based on an assessment of the training material (presentation material and potentially code used in hands-on training sessions), to ensure that:
      • it is technically correct and of sufficient depth and breadth
      • it is compliant with the Foundation’s Trademark Guidelines.
      The CC may seek opinions from representatives of the Foundation’s Technical Steering Committee (TSC).
    • A developer/provider of a Training Module may request Interim Endorsement by contacting the CC via email, giving at least three weeks warning prior to providing the actual material.
    • The CC will provides its assessment within two weeks of receiving the material. The assessment may either be an unconditional endorsement, or an conditional endorsement pending some required changes, or a list of recommended changes for a new revised submission. The assessment may be based on presentation 1 material alone but may also include an examination of any code provided for hands-on training, at the discretion of the CC.
    • All endorsed training modules will be advertised here.
    • Interim Endorsement will be provided for free and to members only.

Products

  • The certification (or endorsement for now) will indicate that a particular product is helpful to the community for building high-assurance seL4-based systems.
  • Guidelines:
    • There are different categories of products that could possibly be certified but will require different treatment:
      • components for seL4-based systems (eg. library, device driver, authentication service)
      • developer tools (eg. compiler, static analysis tool, IDE)
      • seL4-based end-user products
      • Members interested in getting a product endorsed/certified should contact us so we can work with them on developing suitable guidelines/schemes.
